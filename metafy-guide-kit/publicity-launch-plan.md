# Publicity Launch Plan

The guide should not be the only product. It should also be the hub for your deck story.

## Best launch angle

If your main goal is publicity, position the guide like this:

- "I built something different."
- "I tested it seriously."
- "Here is the cleanest place to learn it."

That is stronger than just saying "I made a guide."

## Offer strategy

Best for visibility:

1. Publish a free or low-ticket guide.
2. Make the guide page the home base for updates.
3. Share one useful idea publicly and save the full system for the guide.

## What to post publicly

You want enough info to create interest, but not so much that the guide feels unnecessary.

Good public content:

- Why the deck exists
- One spicy card choice
- One matchup claim
- One clean deck screenshot
- One short clip or sample line

Keep the full breakdown for the guide:

- Full matchup plans
- Mulligan matrix
- Sideboard details
- Sequencing nuance
- Common mistake section

## Launch week plan

### Day 1: Publish

- Post the guide on Metafy.
- Share the deck identity in one sentence.
- Share one screenshot of the list.
- Link directly to the guide.

Post idea:

"I’ve been refining [Deck Name] for [format/set] and finally turned my notes into a guide. The deck is built to [main promise], and I think it has real game into [matchup/meta]. If you want the list, mulligans, and matchup plan, it’s here: [Metafy link]"

### Day 2: Proof

- Share a result, testing number, or matchup record.
- Restate what makes the deck different.

Post idea:

"A big reason I believe in [Deck Name] is that it doesn’t play like the stock lists. The [card/package] changes how the deck fights [matchup], which was the whole point of building it this way."

### Day 3: Teaching moment

- Post one common mistake people make with the deck.
- End with a link back to the guide.

Post idea:

"The biggest mistake with [Deck Name] is [mistake]. People treat it like [wrong identity], but it wins more when you think of it as [correct identity]. I wrote up the full guide and matchup plan here: [Metafy link]"

### Day 5-7: Update

- Add one real update to the guide.
- Announce that the guide is actively maintained.
- Use a major update if the change is meaningful and you want Metafy to notify readers.
- Use a minor update for quiet cleanup or small refinements.

Post idea:

"Updated the [Deck Name] guide after more testing. I added notes on [matchup/card choice/sideboard pivot]. If you’re following the deck, the Metafy page is where I’m keeping everything current: [Metafy link]"

## Positioning tips

- Sell clarity, not just novelty.
- Say what problem the deck solves.
- Give people a reason to believe you tested it seriously.
- Mention updates often. Buyers and free readers both like active guides.
- If the deck is unusual, explain why it is not a meme.

## Publicity stack

If you want more than one channel feeding the guide, use this stack:

- Metafy guide as the main home
- Metafy Posts for update announcements and deck progress
- X/Twitter posts for quick takes and proof points
- Discord posts for direct deck discussion
- Short gameplay clips for credibility
- Optional free one-page cheat sheet as a shareable hook

## Best simple funnel

1. Public post earns curiosity.
2. Metafy guide earns trust.
3. Guide readers follow future updates.
4. Future updates build your name around the deck and whatever comes next.

## Keep momentum alive

You do not need a huge launch. You need repeated signals that:

- the deck is real
- the guide is useful
- you are still refining it

That combination is what usually gets people to share it.
